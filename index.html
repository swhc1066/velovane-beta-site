The problem

Every weather app fails cyclists.

You check the forecast, cross-reference wind speed, guess what 58°F feels like at 17mph on the bike, and hope you picked the right window. Every morning. VeloVane replaces all of that with a single answer: should I ride now, later, or skip today?

What VeloVane does
01

Wind & temperature — the way cyclists think about it

Cycling-specific feels-like temperature. Wind scored for how it actually affects your ride — not just a number and an arrow. Crosswind exposure, wind shifts mid-ride, building vs. dying trends. The data you already check, finally presented for riders.

02

AI that learns how you ride

Set your preferences once — wind threshold, temperature range, preferred times, ride duration. The recommendation engine scores every hour against your profile. After 10 rides, it starts learning from your actual behavior. The advice gets sharper the more you ride.

03

One answer in under 5 seconds

Open the app, see your recommendation: go, wait, or skip. Best ride window highlighted. Tap for detail if you want it, or just trust the call and kit up.

How the beta works

Live ride recommendations, straight to your phone.

For two weeks, you'll receive a daily morning text message with your personalized VeloVane ride recommendation — real weather data for your location, scored against your preferences, delivered in the same voice and format the app will use.

Each message links to a live preview of your VeloVane dashboard — a mobile page showing your full morning forecast, ride windows, and conditions breakdown. Same data, same design, same experience you'll get in the app.

This isn't a survey or a demo video. It's VeloVane working for you, every morning, for 14 days.

Who this is for

Built for riders who ride 3–5 days a week.

VeloVane is designed for cyclists who plan around conditions — not casual riders checking if it might rain. If you think about wind direction before you pick a route, if you've ever skipped a ride because the forecast was ambiguous, if you check three apps before deciding whether to go — you're who we built this for.
